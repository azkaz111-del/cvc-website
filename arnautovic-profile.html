Jelena Zahirovic-Arnautovic
D.O., F.A.C.C.

A passion for comprehensive cardiovascular care, education, and patient advocacy — with an emphasis on prevention, empowerment, and healthy living.

Background

Dr. Arnautovic is a board-certified interventional cardiologist with a passion for comprehensive cardiovascular care, education, and patient advocacy. She earned her Bachelor of Nursing degree from Madonna University and her medical degree from Michigan State University College of Osteopathic Medicine.

She completed her medical training at Henry Ford Health System and is board-certified in interventional cardiology, cardiovascular disease, internal medicine, echocardiography, nuclear cardiology, and vascular interpretation (RPVI).

Roles & Focus

Dr. Arnautovic serves as a Clinical Assistant Professor at Michigan State University College of Osteopathic Medicine, Program Director for the Henry Ford Warren Cardiovascular Fellowship, and Director of the Women's Heart Center at Henry Ford Rochester. She specializes in coronary and peripheral vascular care, with a focus on cardiac critical care, quality improvement, and medical education.

Dedicated to holistic, patient-centered care, she emphasizes prevention, patient empowerment, and healthy living. She is a mentor for the Society for Cardiovascular Angiography and Intervention – Women in Innovation program, and is actively involved in community volunteering and humanitarian efforts.
